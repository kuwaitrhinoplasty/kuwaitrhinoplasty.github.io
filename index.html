A Brief Account of Rhinoplasty Surgery – Procedures, Challenges and Recovery

Introduction to Rhinoplasty

Rhinoplasty, also known as ‘nose job’ in common terms is a corrective plastic surgery to mend any deformity of the nose. The surgery is performed to give the patient a good looking nose which fits his overall facial characteristics.

Nose job has been carried out since ancient times where beauty and facial aesthetics were of prime importance to the people. The term has originated from the Greek word ‘rhino’ meaning nose and ‘plast’ meaning to operate.

The Greeks have been known for their beauty and the aesthetic values throughout the world since ages. The term ‘Greek nose’ has originated from Greece which means the perfect nose with perfectly aligned features. Surprisingly, the Greeks do no associate with this term much.

People all around the world with varied ethnicities vie for the Greek nose and often times ask their surgeons to get unrealistic results. Although it is possible to some extent to correct the nasal features, the patient has to understand that there are many factors which inhibit the surgeon from getting the desired results.

The Problems Corrected by Nose Job

Nose job is recommended for those with functional deformities and inadequacies leading to health issues. Deviated septum is a medical condition where the inner nasal wall separating the nostrils is misaligned causing breathing and other respiratory conditions.

It is also performed on those with severe sinusitis, asthma and chronic bronchitis to soothe the inflammation and ease their discomfort and help them breathe freely.

The surgery can correct various cosmetic defects like humps and bumps on the nasal bridge, hooked, flat, crooked, upturned or celestial nose. The surgery can reduce bulbous nose or can add volume to a skeletal nasal profile. It can also strengthen a weak dorsum which portrays a deformed and droopy nose. Any accidents, wounds, infections or congenital defects can also be corrected through this operation.

An unsatisfactory primary nose job leads the patient to go for a revision Rhinoplasty. Here, the surgeon either does a full-fledged invasive surgery to correct the mistakes or uses non-surgical injectable filler substances to fill in the deficiencies and places left out during the primary Rhinoplasty. In this way the patient can be given a new and healthier looking nose boosting his overall self-image and confidence.

Who Can Perform this Surgery?

Nose job is a complex and delicate procedure requiring keen observation and understanding of the structure of the nose. Every case is unique posing new challenges to the surgeon. The surgeon has to adjust his procedures accordingly and achieve the best results possible for any given case.

The surgeon has to maintain a balance between the aesthetics and the functional aspects of the nose. He has to try and get results in such way that the patient is happy with the shape and size of his nose and yet it suits his overall facial features and ethnic background.

This surgery is performed by an experienced plastic surgeon having years of experience in the field of medicine and surgery. The surgeon should be a practicing cosmetic surgeon with valid license from the medical board of his/her country. He should be permitted from the Government and the Medical Authority of his country to perform surgeries on the patient.

The surgical facility or the clinic where he performs the surgeries should be accredited by the Government and should hold valid license for use of surgical instrument, medicine and any substance used for providing health care to patients.

The surgeon should also have a positive track record of many successful plastic surgeries proving his metal to the prospective patients. As the results of this surgery are instantly visible to everyone, there is no room for error by the surgeon. The patients usually do a thorough background check on the surgeon before committing with him for the surgery.

Funding the Rhinoplasty Surgery

The expenses vary widely throughout the world for this surgery. The patient is required to plan and accumulate the funds in advance. It is also advisable to keep a buffer stock of funds in case of some unduly emergencies during the surgery leading to increase in the overall costs.

The overall costs of a nasal operation can range between $7000 and $10000. If you are flying to other place for the surgery, the costs of travelling and staying will add up to it along with other taxes levied upon you by the Government and medical authorities of that country.

The experience of the surgeon directly affects the costs of the surgery. More the experience more will be his fees. But, you can be assured that you will get the best results possible for you money. It is best to not compromise on this aspect while deciding about Rhinoplasty.

The physical location of the surgical unit or clinic also adds up the overall costs of surgery. If you opt for surgery in a big city you will be bound to pay higher costs for everything ranging from travelling to medical services and taxes.

In certain cases the operation gets complicated and will require the expert advice from other highly experienced surgeons. This will add up to the costs of consulting with them. Any special procedure like cartilage harvesting and grafting will also add up the overall costs.

As the nose goes through surgeries the structure becomes weaker and weaker to take any more surgical incisions. This makes it difficult for the surgeon to perform revision Rhinoplasty. He has to make special arrangements for supporting the weak nasal structure in order to achieve the desired results.

Any complications arising during the surgery and emergency procedures carried out to control the situation will also add up to the overall cost of surgery. Therefore, it is wise to save up on the funds required and keep a buffer stock away in case it is required.

 

Procedure for Rhinoplasty Surgery

Depending on whether it is a normal Rhinoplasty surgery or a non surgical approach, the procedure differs. In the non surgical nose job the surgeon uses filler agents and injects them into the subcutaneous regions of the nose to achieve the desired structure. This procedure is quite simple and takes about 15 to 20 minutes. The patient can walk out after a brief period of rest and can resume his day to day activities immediately.

A surgical Rhinoplasty requires a thorough preparation by the surgeon and the patient. The procedure involves pre-surgery medical checkups, administering of anesthesia, making the incisions, accessing nasal structure, correcting any functional and cosmetic defects, securing the new structure, closing the incisions and finally securing the entire nose with splints placed inside as well as on the nostrils.

 

Recovery from Nose Job

The patient is required to take complete rest for the first few weeks when the wound is still live and is healing. He is advised against any strenuous activity which may put pressure on the delicate nasal walls.

The patient also has to frequently clean any fluids oozing out from the nostrils and has to keep his head in a higher position compared to the rest of the body while sleeping. This keeps the blood from rushing to the head and causing pressure on the nose.

 There are chances of blood pooling inside the nasal cavities and causing hematoma. Therefore, the patient has to keep a check on bleeding by cleaning frequently and avoiding any post-surgical infection.

The nose will start taking new shape over the weeks and will completely settle in into the new structure within a year. You can resume your day to day activities with light exertion after a few weeks when the bleeding has stopped.

 

Final Words on Rhinoplasty Surgery

Rhinoplasty has been giving new hope to patients who felt low and were always conscious and embarrassed about their looks in the social circles. This renewed confidence and an increase in self-worth leads the people to achieve greater things in their career, relationships, business and life in general.

If you are conscious about the way your nose looks or are just unhappy about certain aspect of your nasal structure, this is the right time for consulting with your surgeon and deciding on the dates for going through the surgery. If you have done your research properly on the surgeon and the time of the year for surgery, you will reap the rewards of a perfectly fitting nose to your facial traits.
